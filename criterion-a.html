Criterion A – Investigating

Themes, symbolism and visual culture

Paragraph 1 — Themes, Symbolism & Visual Culture

To start my research, I looked into how visual things like symbols, colours, and patterns can show emotions—especially happiness, change, and personal identity. I focused on simple symbols such as butterflies, flowers, and hearts because they all represent ideas like growth, beauty, and connection. Butterflies reminded me of transformation, while floral designs felt soft and peaceful. I also checked what different colours make people feel, and shades like lavender and pastels stood out because they’re calming and gentle. All this helped me build my mood board and guided the style and mood of my mini dress and corset design.

Paragraph 2 — Movements & Designers (Marimekko + Finnish Design)

I also explored Marimekko and general Finnish design, which focus a lot on happiness found in everyday life, nature, and simple, bold patterns. Their designs are cheerful but not overwhelming, and that influenced how I used floral details and clean shapes. The Finnish idea of “Sisu,” which means inner strength and resilience, also connected to how nature-based imagery can express emotional well-being. Even though my final design is more romantic and couture-inspired, these ideas shaped the way I thought about symbols and the story my design communicates.

Paragraph 3 — Happiness, Clothing & Emotional Design

Another part of my research was understanding how clothing can affect emotions. The KonMari method inspired me because it links clothing with joy and comfort. This is why I experimented with soft fabrics like lace, organza, and satin—they naturally feel gentle and expressive. I also learned that texture, shape, and colour can show emotions without words. Flowing fabrics can represent freedom, floral details can show purity, and touches of gold can feel warm and uplifting. All of this helped me create a design meant to express happiness through visual elements.
