Criterion C – Creating

Artwork development & realisation

Title & Concept of the Artwork

Title: Phoenix Ascendant
The idea behind Phoenix Ascendant is rooted in transformation, resilience, and emotional rebirth. Inspired by phoenix mythology, Vietnamese motifs, and haute couture, the artwork visualizes rising above adversity. Its flowing silhouette, colour gradients, and hand-embroidered motifs work together to express a powerful journey from struggle to empowerment. The phoenix is used not just as a mythical creature, but as a metaphor for personal evolution, identity, and growth.

Process of Artwork — Beginning Stage

The early stage focused on translating sketches into physical explorations. Organza, satin, and silk were chosen for their contrasting properties—lightness, transparency, and structure. Draping tests on a mannequin helped study organza movement and ensure the layered fabrics could visually “lift,” resembling rising flames or wings. Dye tests explored gradient transitions from icy blue to deep purple to reflect emotional and spiritual evolution. The phoenix motif was tested in various positions on bodice and skirt to determine the strongest narrative direction.

Process of Artwork — Middle Stage

The middle stage refined silhouette structure, colour transitions, and motif execution. Organza layers were bias-cut for better movement, while the satin base was shaped for stability. Phoenix motifs were hand-embroidered with silk threads and crystals to enhance luminosity. Techniques like ombré dyeing, layered embroidery, and light-mapping previewed how crystals would sparkle under movement. Transparency and opacity were balanced carefully so motifs emerged naturally from the layers.

Process of Artwork — End Stage Realisation

The final stage involved assembling and refining the gown for balance, accuracy, and emotional impact. Adjustments were made to silhouette, motif placement, crystal distribution, and organza layering to ensure harmony. In the finished garment, gradient colours express emotional evolution, embroidery symbolizes rebirth, and layered fabrics reflect flight, freedom, and growth.

Materials, Techniques & Processes

Materials:
Organza, satin, silk thread, and crystal embellishments. These materials interact as mixed media—organza adds lightness, satin offers structure, and crystals create reflective highlights.

Tools & Application:
• Fabric shears and rotary cutters for shaping • Fine needles for hand embroidery • Fabric glue and stitching for crystals • Gradient dipping and brushes for ombré dyeing

Techniques:
• Ombre dyeing for smooth transitions • Bias cutting for fabric flow • Hand embroidery for raised texture • Layering for depth and dimension • Crystal embellishment for emotional luminosity

Colour Choices:
Icy blue, violet, deep purple, and crystal highlights. Light colours at the hem transition to deeper tones at the bodice, symbolizing ascension. Analogous colours create harmony, while sparkles brighten the foreground. Cool tones reflect struggle; purples express empowerment.

Connection to Art Movement & Visual Choice Explanation

The artwork aligns closely with Contemporary Haute Couture, known for craftsmanship, symbolic storytelling, and structural innovation. Hand embroidery, dramatic silhouettes, layered textures, and narrative-focused design place Phoenix Ascendant firmly within this movement.

Visual Choice Explanation:
• Colours: gradients symbolize emotional rebirth • Forms: flowing silhouette suggests flight and upliftment • Composition: motifs guide the viewer’s gaze upward • Emotion: communicates empowerment and transformation • Texture: crystals add brilliance, embroidery adds depth • Movement: organza layers create natural motion Together, these choices express an emotionally charged story celebrating identity and transformation.
