Criterion B – Developing Ideas

Exploring materials, forms and techniques

Paragraph 1 — Exploring Materials, Forms & Techniques

Using my mood board as a guide, I tried out different materials to see how they affected the look and feeling of the design. I tested lace, organza, satin, and chiffon to check their movement and softness. I also sketched different shapes, especially butterfly wings, floral lines, and flowing dress silhouettes. This helped me practice smooth curves, symmetry, and natural shapes. I tried different colour samples—lavender, dusty blue, whites, and golds—to see how they layered together and what emotions they created.

Methods I used included:

  • Sketching flowers, wings, silhouettes, and colour blends
  • Testing fabric textures and layering
  • Trying digital floral patterns and painted surface designs
  • Mapping symbols and developing ideas based on personal meaning and references

Paragraph 2 — Evolution of Ideas

As I tried different things, I realized that softer fabrics and nature-inspired designs worked best for the emotional message I wanted to give. My early ideas were more literal, but they slowly became more stylized and romantic. Repeating butterflies and floral elements helped build a clear theme of transformation and gentleness. Keeping to a consistent pastel palette made the overall mood feel calm and connected.
